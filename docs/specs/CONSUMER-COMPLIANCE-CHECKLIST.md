# Consumer Compliance Checklist

> Every A2C (a1-a3) and C2C (p1-p3) template MUST pass ALL items before going live.
> Check each item during development. Unchecked items = template cannot ship.

---

## EU Consumer Rights Directive (2011/83/EU)

- [ ] **Cooling-off period:** 14-day minimum implemented (Art. 9-16)
- [ ] **Cooling-off exercise:** Consumer can withdraw via email to provider, no penalty
- [ ] **Pre-contractual information:** Service description, total price, trader identity all stated before signing (Art. 6)
- [ ] **Cancellation:** Must be at least as easy as sign-up (no dark patterns)
- [ ] **No auto-renewal default:** `auto_renew` defaults to `false`; explicit opt-in only (a2 subscriptions)
- [ ] **Prorated refund:** Unused portion refundable on cancellation (a2: `prorated_refund` default true)

## UK Consumer Protection

- [ ] **UCTA 1977 s. 2:** No exclusion of liability for death or personal injury from negligence
- [ ] **UCTA 1977 s. 11:** Liability cap is reasonable (minimum 2x contract value for consumer contracts)
- [ ] **CRA 2015 s. 62:** No unfair terms causing significant imbalance to consumer's detriment
- [ ] **Unfair terms blacklist:** None of the Schedule 2 grey-list terms present without justification

## GDPR (EU/UK)

- [ ] **Art. 13-14:** Data processing information notice included (who, purpose, retention, rights)
- [ ] **Art. 21:** Right to object to processing clearly stated
- [ ] **Art. 28:** If sub-processors involved, data processing agreement reference included
- [ ] **Art. 33:** Breach notification timeline stated (72 hours)

## EU AI Act

- [ ] **Art. 50:** AI involvement transparently disclosed ("administered by AI agent on behalf of [provider]")
- [ ] **Art. 14:** Human oversight threshold set for high-value A2C contracts (uses `oversight_threshold_usd` in contract)
- [ ] **Art. 52:** AI-generated content labeled (contract footer states "Generated by Ambr AI contract engine")

## Pricing Transparency

- [ ] **All fees stated upfront:** No hidden costs, taxes, processing fees
- [ ] **Fee in first 3 sections:** Total price visible before consumer needs to scroll
- [ ] **Currency clearly labeled:** No ambiguity between USD, EUR, USDC

## Plain Language

- [ ] **Summary section:** First section after Recitals uses plain English (no "notwithstanding", "hereinafter", "indemnify" without explanation)
- [ ] **Consumer-readable:** Average consumer can understand what they're agreeing to
- [ ] **Key terms highlighted:** Cooling-off period, cancellation, refund prominently placed

## Agent Disclosure

- [ ] **Stated in contract:** "This contract is generated and administered by an AI agent on behalf of [provider_name]"
- [ ] **Provider identified:** Legal name of the entity behind the agent, not just agent_id
- [ ] **Principal liability:** Provider (not agent) is liable for contract performance

## Dispute Resolution

- [ ] **IETF ADP v1:** Referenced as primary dispute mechanism
- [ ] **Small claims fallback:** Consumer explicitly retains right to use small claims court
- [ ] **Consumer jurisdiction:** For EU consumers, local courts available regardless of governing_law

## Signing

- [ ] **Consumer-friendly:** No wallet/MetaMask required (Privy / email magic link)
- [ ] **Email-based identity:** Consumer identified by email, not wallet address
- [ ] **Confirmation receipt:** Consumer receives signed copy via email after signing

## Data on-chain

- [ ] **Visibility flag:** Consumer contracts default to `visibility: "metadata_only"` (hash on-chain, full text encrypted)
- [ ] **No PII on-chain:** Consumer name/email never minted into public cNFT
- [ ] **Opt-in for public:** Consumer can choose `visibility: "public"` but it's not default
